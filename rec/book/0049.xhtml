READING SKILL Distinguishing Facts from Speculation Scientific and historical texts often contain a mix of both facts and speculation. Facts are ideas that are known to be true, or that can be proven. For example, the idea that Ötzi died over 5,300 years ago (paragraph A) is considered a fact because it can be proven by carbon dating. Speculation refers to ideas that have not been proven to be true or false. Words that indicate speculation include think, believe, may, might, could, possibly, probably, perhaps, and (un)likely.
