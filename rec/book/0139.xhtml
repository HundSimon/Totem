READING SKILL Making Inferences A reading text does not always state everything directly. Sometimes you need to “read between the lines” to find—or infer—the meaning. You can infer meaning by using your knowledge of the topic, clues and hints in the text, and common sense. An inference is a kind of “smart guess.” Making inferences while reading allows the reader to reach a deeper level of meaning. MAKING INFERENCES A. Look back at paragraphs B–D of Reading B.
