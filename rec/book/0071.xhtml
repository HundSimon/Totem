DESIGNED FOR FLIGHT Over millions of years, pterosaurs, bats, and birds adapted the same five arm bones (color-coded here) into three different wing designs. The pteroid bone is only found in pterosaur fossils; it helped strengthen the front part of its wing. A very long fourth finger supported the wing. Three small, clawed fingers Air sacs inside the wing bones made them very light.
