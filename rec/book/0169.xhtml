READING SKILL Understanding a Research Summary When writers describe an experiment or a piece of research, they often cover the following points: the purpose of the study (the question they want to answer) the method (how they set up and carried out the study) the results (what the study found) the conclusion (the significance of the results) When reading a research summary, highlighting these points and noting them in the margin can help your understanding.
