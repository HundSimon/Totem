READING SKILL Recognizing Contrastive Relationships Writers use certain words and phrases to indicate a contrast between ideas presented in a text. It is important to identify and understand these phrases in order to fully comprehend the passage. The words and phrases in bold below are common examples. I’m a good swimmer, but / yet I rarely go swimming. I’m a good swimmer. However, I rarely go swimming. Though / Although / Even though I’m afraid of the water, I went swimming. I went swimming though / although / even though I’m afraid of the water. I went swimming in the sea despite / in spite of the bad weather.
